Welcome to the Huntington
Research Division

The Huntington was founded as a research institution and has nurtured an active intellectual community since Mr. Huntington's day. Each year, nearly 2,000 scholars from all over the world use the research facilities in American and British history, literature, and art, and in botany and horticulture. Many laud the facilities, setting, collections, and community of scholars as forming the best research environment in the world.

Scholars tackle issues that are in the forefront of scholarship today – ranging from women's studies, to exploration and discovery of the Americas. Fellowships are available for scholars from graduate students through senior professors. The results of research are shared through lectures, seminars, conferences, and publications. The oldest book publisher in Southern California, the Huntington issues a scholarly journal, catalogs, bibliographies, and monographs on a variety of subjects.
