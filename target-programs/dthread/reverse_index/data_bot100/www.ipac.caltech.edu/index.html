Top Stories

2MASS Team Wins Muhlmann Award - The team members of the Two Micron All-Sky Survey (2MASS) have been named recipients of the Maria and Eric Muhlmann Award for 2006. The award was announced May 22 by the Astronomical Society of the Pacific. +  Learn More


NASA's Spitzer Space Telescope has snapped a picture of the bits and pieces making up Comet 73P/Schwassman-Wachmann 3, which is continuing to break apart on its periodic journey around the sun. The observatory's infrared view provides the first look at the dusty trail left by the disintegrating comet after it splintered apart in 1995. +  Learn More



Featured Presentation

Many objects in the universe are hidden by thick clouds of gas and dust, or are too cool to be seen by optical telescopes. Infrared telescopes can detect the cooler objects in space and can "see" through these dense clouds. Learn about infrared light and what it reveals about a variety of objects in the universe by viewing "The Hidden Universe".


IPAC Legacy Gallery Highlight

Cygnus, also known as the northern cross, is a constellation in the northern hemisphere. This image from the Midcourse Space Experiment shows the galactic plane as it cuts through a portion of the constellation. The glowing filaments are clouds of dust and gas between the stars. The bright compact regions are areas of star formation.     + Learn More


Education Highlight

Teachers and Students Discover a Cauldron of Star Formation - With less than two hours of observations from NASA's Spitzer Space Telescope, teachers and students from the Spitzer Space Telescope Research Program for Teachers and Students, nearly quadrupled the number of known stars in a small portion of the Witch Head's Nebula. Now they have been granted additional time on Spitzer to scan the entire nebula for stellar embryos.     + Learn More.
