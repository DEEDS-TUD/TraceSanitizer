My lifelong goal is to develop and apply technologies to better understand the nature of human existence, and to engineer improvements thereupon. I am currently researching 1) neural engineering and bioengineering applications of light-controllable ion channels, 2) the causal role of particular neuron types in neural computation, and 3) how neural circuits selectively engage plasticity mechanisms for specific memories. At Stanford I currently work chiefly with Richard Tsien, Jennifer Raymond, Karl Deisseroth, and Mark Schnitzer.
