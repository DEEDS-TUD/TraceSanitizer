Paul Jennings, who has been on the Caltech campus as a student, professor, and administrator for 44 years, returned to the Provost position in 2004 after a nine-year hiatus. He served as Vice President and Provost from 1989 to 1995. Thus, he was the Institute's sixth, and is now its eighth, Provost since the post was created in 1962.

Jennings, who is highly regarded within the Caltech community for his energy, enthusiasm, and organizational skills, is also an international figure in earthquake engineering and seismology. An expert in the earthquake response of structures and in the characteristics of strong earthquake motions, he has played an active role in investigating the effects of damaging earthquakes. He has been the president of the Seismological Society of America and of the Earthquake Engineering Research Institute. He was a member of the National Research Council's Committee on Seismology and chair of the Committee on Natural Disasters. Recently he has served as Chair of the California Council on Science and Technology and chaired the National Research Council Committee on Criteria for the Management of Los Alamos and Lawrence Livermore National Laboratories.

Jennings earned a B.S. from Colorado State University in 1958, a M.S. from Caltech in 1960, and a Ph.D. from Caltech in 1963. From 1963 to 1965, he served on the teaching staff of the U.S. Air Force Academy. In 1965, he became a research fellow at Caltech in civil engineering and swiftly moved up the academic ladder to become a full professor in 1972. He served as Chair of Caltech's Division of Engineering and Applied Science from 1985 to 1989, and as the Acting Vice President for Business and Finance in 1995, and again in 1998-99.

Jennings is a hiker and avid fly fisherman. He and his wife Missy have four grown daughters: Theresa, Kathryn, Jennifer, and Maren.
